# Chapter 30: Arc IV — Zollverein Independent

> *"The Anarchs do not want to seize the domain. They want to leave it. They want Zollverein to be its own domain, answerable to no Prince, bound by no foundation charter. They do not know that Shaft 12 is inside Zollverein, and that the wardens will not let them have it."*
> — Dieter Kranz, Sheriff of Essen

---

## THREAT ASSESSMENT: ZOLLVEREIN INDEPENDENT

*Scenario:* The Anarch movement — led by Rika Bohm, sustained by the Pragmatist faction's institutional infiltration — makes a formal proposal to the Primogen Council. Zeche Zollverein, the UNESCO World Heritage site in Katernberg, should be recognized as an independent Anarch domain, governed by the Kindred who live and work there, free of Camarilla protocol and Camarilla obligation. The proposal is legally plausible — the Stiftung Zollverein is a legally autonomous foundation, and its Kindred board members are not formally acknowledged by the Essen domain. The proposal is politically explosive — Zollverein contains Shaft 12, and Shaft 12 is the deepest access to the anomaly, and the wardens will not surrender it to anyone.

---

## ACT ONE: THE PROPOSAL

Rika Bohm presents the proposal at a public Council session. She is prepared, articulate, and supported by documentation the Anarchs have spent years assembling — the Stiftung's charter, the domain's acknowledgment protocols, the Camarilla's own precedents for recognizing breakaway domains. The legal argument is stronger than anyone expected. The political argument — that Essen's Camarilla has governed the city into decline, and that the Anarchs can govern Zollverein better — is emotionally compelling even to Kindred who consider it practically naive.

The Council is divided. The Ventrue Primogen opposes the proposal on principle. The Toreador Primogen (Eva's representative, not Eva herself) considers it culturally interesting. The Brujah Primogen — Kranz, who has known Bohm for twenty years — is silent. The Nosferatu Primogen — Voss, still communicating through written single-word votes — submits a "Nein" with no explanation. Brenner abstains. The proposal is neither accepted nor rejected. It is deferred for further study. The deferral is a victory for the Anarchs and a disaster for everyone else.

The coterie is asked to investigate — by the Seneschal, who wants to know how strong the Anarchs' legal case is, or by the Sheriff, who wants to know whether the Anarchs understand what Zollverein contains, or by the wardens, who have never asked the coterie for anything before and whose request is terrifying because it exists at all.

---

## ACT TWO: THE STAKES

The investigation reveals the stakes. Zollverein is not merely a cultural site the Anarchs want to govern. Zollverein is the surface access to Shaft 12, the deepest vertical penetration of the anomaly's perimeter, the point where the containment structure is thinnest, the location where the 2024 scan detected movement. Zollverein is the wardens' most critical surface asset. They will not surrender it to anyone, least of all to a political movement whose ideology includes the proposition that the Camarilla's institutional architecture has failed and should be dismantled.

The Anarchs do not know about Shaft 12 — not in detail. They know the mine exists, they know it was sealed in 1986, they know the wardens maintain some kind of presence there. They do not know about the anomaly, the containment, the scan, the signal, the thing that has been sealed beneath the Ruhr since before the city existed. If they learn — if the coterie tells them, or if the wardens are forced to demonstrate why Shaft 12 cannot be surrendered — the Anarch movement will fracture. Bohm will be forced to choose between her political project and the reality of what Zollverein's subsurface contains. The Pragmatists will splinter. The Revisionists will demand disclosure. The Underground Faction will side with the wardens, and the Anarchs will discover that their Nosferatu contacts were never allies.

The wardens are waiting. They have not intervened because intervening would acknowledge the Anarchs' proposal as a genuine threat, and the wardens do not acknowledge political threats. They are waiting for the domain to resolve the crisis. If the domain cannot resolve it — if the Council defers indefinitely, or if the Anarchs' legal case advances to the point of threatening Shaft 12 — the wardens will resolve it themselves.

---

## ACT THREE: THE RESOLUTION

**Option A: Negotiate.** The coterie brokers an agreement between the Anarchs, the Council, and the wardens. Zollverein receives a measure of administrative autonomy — the Anarchs govern the surface cultural site, the wardens retain subsurface access, the domain's institutional architecture is adjusted to accommodate both. The agreement requires concessions from every party and trust from none. It is fragile, provisional, and the most sophisticated political work the coterie will ever do. If it holds, Essen becomes a model for Anarch-Camarilla coexistence. If it fails, the wardens intervene.

**Option B: Tell the Anarchs.** The coterie tells Bohm what Zollverein's subsurface contains — the anomaly, the scan, the wardens, the containment. Bohm must choose: abandon the independence project to protect the seals, or continue the project knowing what it threatens. Most Bohms choose correctly. The Bohm who chooses incorrectly destroys the Anarch movement and forces the wardens to act.

**Option C: Let the Wardens Act.** The coterie does nothing. The Council defers. The Anarchs advance. The wardens intervene — not violently, not publicly, but definitively. Bohm disappears. The Anarch Pragmatist faction collapses. The Council discovers that the wardens have been capable of this all along, and that their restraint has been a choice, and that the choice has now been withdrawn. The domain survives, at the cost of discovering that its governance is conditional on the wardens' tolerance.

**Option D: Descend with Bohm.** The coterie takes Bohm into the underground — to Shaft 12, to the seal, to the wardens' observation post. Bohm sees what the wardens are guarding. Bohm makes a choice. The choice changes the Anarch movement and the domain and, possibly, the Something's relationship to the surface. The Something has been listening to the city's music. Now it listens to an Anarch organizer who has just learned what the city is built on.

---

## NPC MOTIVATION MAP

**Rika Bohm:** Wants Zollverein independent because she believes the Camarilla has failed Essen and the Anarchs can govern better. Does not know what Zollverein contains. Her reaction to learning will define the arc's outcome.

**Dieter Kranz:** Torn between his duty as Sheriff and his respect for Bohm's argument. Will enforce the Council's decision but will not enforce a decision that destroys the Anarchs for reasons they were never told.

**Nosferatu Wardens:** Will not surrender Shaft 12. Will not negotiate on Shaft 12. Will not explain Shaft 12. Their patience is finite.

**Friedrich von Hügel:** Watching. Knows the wardens will intervene. Has not told anyone. The coterie's audience with Friedrich — if they request one — will produce an explanation so oblique it qualifies as a test.

---

*Zollverein is the axis on which the domain's political future turns. The Anarchs' proposal is not unreasonable. Zollverein is a legally autonomous site governed by an autonomous foundation whose Kindred are not formally acknowledged. The Anarchs' case is sound. The case does not account for Shaft 12. The wardens do not care about the case.*

— K.W.
