## German Terminology

**Aalto-Theater** (*AHL-toh tay-AH-ter*): Essen's opera house, opened 1988. Primary Toreador Elysium and the source of the Opera Phenomenon — music that transmits through the underground during performances. The building's foundation sits above one of the oldest mapped tunnel intersections.

**Bauordnungsamt** (*BOW-ord-nungs-ahmt*): Municipal building regulations office. A source of maps, permits, and institutional friction. Several ghouls are embedded in its records division.

**das Horchen** (*dahs HOR-khen*): "The listening." The state characters enter when their Willpower reaches zero in Tier Four of the underground. Passive, receptive, permanently altered.

**die Alte** (*dee AHL-tuh*): "The old one." A term whispered in the Ghost Station, never explained. Refers to something below. The wardens do not use this term. The wardens use no term at all.

**die Verpflichtung** (*dee fair-PFLICH-tung*): "The obligation." The warden oath, administered to Nosferatu who accept the duty of seal maintenance. Its text is not recorded.

**Gildehof Center** (*GIL-duh-hof*): The Seneschal's administrative headquarters. Offices, archives, access to the Ghost Station. The bunker beneath houses the warden operations centre — not accessible through the public building.

**Grenze** (*GREN-tsuh*): "Boundary." The marked line in Tier Three beyond which the wardens do not patrol. Crossing it removes warden support permanently.

**Grillo-Theater** (*GRILL-oh*): Essen's oldest theatre, opened 1892. Secondary Elysium. The building sits on the site of a pre-industrial mine sealed in 1820.

**Königsallee** (*KER-nihgs-ah-lay*): Düsseldorf's premier boulevard. Metaphorically, the aesthetic standard by which Essen is judged and found provincial. The Toreador of both cities maintain an elaborate mutual criticism that functions as diplomacy.

**Nordviertel** (*NORD-feer-tel*): Northern district, formerly industrial, now mixed-use. Contains the grate through which a Gangrel character may have first heard the breathing.

**Ruhrgebiet** (*ROOR-guh-beet*): The Ruhr region as a whole — the dense urban corridor of interconnected cities from Duisburg to Dortmund. The *Ruhrpott* is the colloquial form, carrying connotations of industrial identity and working-class solidarity.

**Saalbau** (*ZAHL-bow*): The underground connection between the Sheraton Hotel and the Aalto-Theater complex. Functions as a Nosferatu listening post and access route to the deep network.

**Schachtmeister** (*SHAHKT-my-ster*): "Shaft master." The Nosferatu Primogen of Essen. A title, a burden, and — possibly — something that was once Kindred and has become something else through prolonged contact with the deep.

**Stiftung** (*SHTIFT-ung*): Foundation. The legal instrument through which much of Essen's domain governance is conducted. The Stiftung Zollverein manages the Zollverein complex. The Stiftung Krupp manages the industrial legacy. Both contain board seats occupied by ghouls and Municipalist-aligned Kindred.

**Strukturwandel** (*shtrook-TOOR-vahn-del*): "Structural change." The post-industrial transition that reshaped the Ruhr's economy and identity. In the domain's usage, the term also refers to the institutional transition that replaced industrial governance with cultural governance — the shift that made the Municipalists possible.

**Verschlossen** (*fair-SHLOSS-en*): "Closed" or "sealed." The word on the plaques marking Nosferatu-maintained seal chambers in Tier Three. A statement of fact that is also a warning.

**Zeche Zollverein** (*TSEH-khuh TSOL-fair-eye-n*): The Zollverein Coal Mine Industrial Complex. UNESCO World Heritage Site, Municipalist headquarters, and the surface location of the Shaft 12 concrete seal. The most beautiful tomb in the world.

## VtM 5e Terminology in Domain Context

**Acknowledgment:** The formal process by which a Kindred is recognised as present within the Essen Domain. Provisional for sixty nights; requires renewal or permanent application thereafter. Administered by the Seneschal's office. Failure to complete acknowledgment is grounds for expulsion.

**Boon:** A debt of obligation between Kindred. In Essen, boons are recorded in the Gildehof ledger, maintained by the Seneschal, and may be called decades — or centuries — after they were incurred. The lost ledger volume (Arc III) contains boons that predate the surviving records.

**Domain:** The territory ruled by the Prince — in Essen's case, the city and its immediate surroundings. The domain's borders are not formally defined; the Ruhr's interlocking cities make hard borders impractical.

**Elysium:** Neutral ground where violence is forbidden. Essen has three: the Ghost Station (primary), the Grillo-Theater (secondary), and the Museum Folkwang (cultural). The Keeper of Elysium, Eva Sonnenberg, administers access and enforces the peace.

**Embrace:** The act of transforming a mortal into a Kindred. Friedrich has never sired — or has never acknowledged siring. This is not unusual for a Ventrue of his generation. It is carefully noted regardless.

**Kindred:** The proper term for what mortals call vampires. Used universally in the dossier. "Vampire" appears only in quotations or when describing mortal usage.

**Masquerade:** The concealment of Kindred existence from mortal awareness. In Essen, the Masquerade is reinforced by the domain's institutional architecture — foundations that manage public perception, cultural institutions that redirect curiosity, and the systematic exclusion of digital technology from Kindred communication.

**Primogen:** The council of clan elders who advise the Prince. Essen's Primogen consists of five seats — Ventrue, Toreador, Tremere, Nosferatu, and Brujah. The Nosferatu seat has never been occupied by a physically present Schachtmeister in living memory.

**Second Inquisition (SI):** The coalition of government agencies and religious hunters who discovered Kindred existence through digital surveillance. Vienna fell to the SI. Essen has not — the domain's digital silence and institutional camouflage have been effective. The SI's attention is not fixed on Essen, but it could be.
