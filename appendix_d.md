# Appendix D: The Ruhr Network

**Classification:** Both

> *"The Ruhr is not a city. It is a chain of cities that share infrastructure, labour markets, and — for a very long time — the same geological formation. The Kindred of the Ruhr understand this better than their mortal counterparts. The network described below is informal, unwritten, and older than the Camarilla's formal borders. Treat it as a reference for travel, alliance, and the question the Schachtmeister has never answered: whether the anomaly beneath Essen has analogues beneath the cities that surround it."*
> — Dr. Katharina Weiss, orientation supplement

---

## The Ruhr Kindred Network

The four neighbouring domains — Dortmund, Bochum, Duisburg, and Düsseldorf — form a loose association with Essen, bound by proximity, shared mortal infrastructure, and an unspoken recognition that the Ruhr's Kindred are too few and too intertwined to afford open conflict. No formal treaty governs inter-domain relations. The understanding is older than treaties and more fragile.

Inter-domain travel follows protocols established after Vienna — digital silence, physical documentation, advance notification through intermediaries. A Kindred travelling from Essen to Dortmund carries a letter of introduction from the Seneschal's office. The letter is opened, read, and returned. No copy is made. The domain receiving the visitor registers their presence through institutional memory rather than documentation.

## Dortmund

**Governance:** A Brujah Prince, Markus Reiter, who seized Praxis in 2008 after the previous Ventrue administration collapsed under post-Vienna paranoia. His rule is direct, personal, and increasingly strained by the demands of managing a domain without the institutional infrastructure Essen relies on.

**Sect Balance:** Camarilla-controlled, but with strong Anarch presence in the northern districts and a growing Independent movement centred on the university's academic Kindred. The Tremere maintain a small chantry in the university district.

**Dominant Clans:** Brujah (ruling), Nosferatu (significant warden presence — Dortmund sits on the eastern edge of the same geological formation as Essen), Gangrel (unusual concentration for an urban domain).

**Relationship to Essen:** Respectful distance. Reiter admires Friedrich's institutional achievement but considers it a Ventrue indulgence — governance should be personal, visible, accountable. He has not visited Essen since 2012. His Nosferatu have been visiting regularly. He does not discuss what they report.

**Unique Feature:** The Dortmund-Ems Canal system contains submerged Kindred havens — Gangrel-maintained underwater sanctuaries accessible only by descent. The Nosferatu have been investigating the canal bed for decades. They have found formations consistent with the Essen deep spaces. They have not publicised this.

## Bochum

**Governance:** A Primogen Council without a Prince — the domain's Ventrue elder entered torpor in 1997 and the Council has governed by consensus since, a situation that works better than anyone expected and worse than anyone admits. The Seneschal, Gustav Kern (Ventrue), administers daily affairs with a competence that makes the Prince's absence functionally irrelevant — a fact that Essen's Municipalists study with great interest.

**Sect Balance:** Camarilla dominant, with a small but persistent Anarch faction centred around the university's social sciences departments. The Anarchs find Bochum's consensus governance frustrating — there is no single authority to oppose.

**Dominant Clans:** Ventrue (institutional control), Toreador (cultural legitimacy via the Bergbaumuseum), Nosferatu (minimal — Bochum has no significant underground network).

**Relationship to Essen:** Closest ally. Kern and Katharina Weiss correspond regularly, and the Bochum-Essen corridor is the most active inter-domain route in the Ruhr. Bochum's Primogen Council has been observing Essen's institutional model for decades, and several of Friedrich's innovations have been adopted — in modified form — in Bochum.

**Unique Feature:** The Deutsches Bergbau-Museum (German Mining Museum) in Bochum houses the most comprehensive collection of mining records in Germany — including documents the Essen Nosferatu have been attempting to access for decades. The museum's archives contain references to "anomalous geological findings" in the Zollverein workings that were classified by the Krupp administration in 1923 and have never been declassified. Dr. Lena Vogler is a research affiliate. She has been asking questions about Essen.

## Duisburg

**Governance:** Anarch-controlled since 2011. The Baron, a Brujah named Liesel Brandt, took the city after the Camarilla administration collapsed during the Euro crisis. She runs a domain that is functional, efficient, and increasingly aware that Duisburg's industrial decline has left it without the resources to sustain an independent Kindred population.

**Sect Balance:** Anarch-dominated. The Camarilla maintain no formal presence. The Nosferatu maintain informal presence — Duisburg's harbour district contains tunnel access points that connect to the deeper Ruhr network, and the wardens have never explained why they monitor them.

**Dominant Clans:** Brujah (ruling), Gangrel (harbour territory), Nosferatu (underground monitoring), Caitiff (unusual concentration — Duisburg accepts those Essen would not).

**Relationship to Essen:** Complicated. Brandt and Kranz have mutual respect from the 1980s union struggles, but Brandt considers Friedrich's institutional model a form of undead feudalism. The Duisburg Anarchs use Essen as a cautionary example — a domain where the Kindred serve the institutions rather than the other way around. The Schachtmeister's wardens maintain contact with Duisburg's Nosferatu through the tunnel network. The surface domains know nothing about this.

**Unique Feature:** Duisburg's inland harbour is the largest in the world. It contains submerged infrastructure — docks, locks, sunken barges — that predate the current city and may predate the Camarilla. The Nosferatu have identified signatures in the harbour bed consistent with the anomaly's geological profile. They have not informed the Baron.

## Düsseldorf

**Governance:** A Toreador Prince, Marius von Falkenberg, whose Praxis has been uninterrupted since 1902. His rule is a study in applied aesthetics — Düsseldorf's Kindred politics are conducted through artistic patronage, fashion, and the social architecture of the *Königsallee*. The domain's Camarilla status is unquestioned because no one has ever found a reason to question it. Von Falkenberg is a master of making power look like taste.

**Sect Balance:** Camarilla dominant. The Anarchs are a cultural movement rather than a political threat — Düsseldorf Anarchs critique the Prince's tastes rather than his Praxis.

**Dominant Clans:** Toreador (ruling), Ventrue (financial sector), Tremere (academic presence at the university), Nosferatu (minimal — Düsseldorf has not been a mining city since before the Camarilla).

**Relationship to Essen:** Cordial but cool. Düsseldorf considers Essen provincial — a industrial city that dressed itself in culture too late to be convincing. Essen considers Düsseldorf superficial — a city that has never had to dig for anything. The two Princes maintain formal relations through intermediaries. Friedrich has not visited Düsseldorf since 1903. Von Falkenberg last visited Essen in 1952, for the opening of the Grillo-Theater's postwar restoration. He did not return.

**Unique Feature:** Düsseldorf has no underground — the city's geology prevented the extensive mining that defined the rest of the Ruhr. This fact, which the Düsseldorf Kindred treat as evidence of their domain's superiority, is being re-examined. The Schachtmeister's wardens have been mapping the anomaly's perimeter for two hundred years. The perimeter extends beyond Essen's borders — beneath Bochum, into Duisburg, toward Dortmund. It does not extend toward Düsseldorf. The anomaly respects geological boundaries. The Schachtmeister has advised the Primogen Council — in writing, without elaboration — that Düsseldorf's absence from the anomaly's footprint is "a matter requiring further study." The study is ongoing.

## Inter-Domain Protocol

Kindred travelling between Ruhr domains observe the following, which have the force of tradition rather than law:

1. **Written introduction required.** A letter from the ruling authority of the departing domain, presented on arrival. The letter is returned after inspection. No copies are retained, officially.
2. **Feeding rights are not transferable.** A Kindred acknowledged in Essen must secure separate feeding rights in any neighbouring domain. Negotiation is conducted through the respective Seneschals' offices. The process takes two to five nights.
3. **Digital silence is universal.** The prohibition extends across domain borders. A Kindred who transmits digital communication from one Ruhr city to another violates the protocols of both domains.
4. **The underground is never discussed.** No formal agreement enforces this. It is simply understood. Ruhr Kindred who mention the deep spaces in inter-domain conversation find the conversation ending. They do not repeat the mistake.

## The Anomaly Question

The Schachtmeister's wardens have been mapping the anomaly's subsurface extent for over two centuries. The perimeter forms an irregular ellipse approximately eight kilometres by five kilometres, centred on the Zollverein complex. The ellipse extends into Bochum's northern districts, Duisburg's eastern edge, and Dortmund's western periphery. The wardens have identified formations consistent with the deep spaces in all three cities — sealed galleries that predate the mines, ventilation shafts that descend beyond any documented depth, acoustic signatures that match the breathing's frequency.

The wardens have not informed the neighbouring domains. The formal justification is that the information would destabilise inter-domain relations without improving containment capacity. The actual justification — which the Schachtmeister has recorded in the sealed archive and which Katharina has read — is that the wardens do not know what would happen if multiple domains attempted to intervene in the anomaly simultaneously. The anomaly has been responding to a single set of seals, maintained by a single warden system, for six hundred years. Introducing additional variables — additional wardens, additional Princes, additional political agendas — might accelerate the awakening rather than prevent it. The Schachtmeister's recommendation: "Maintain the perimeter. Observe the neighbours. Pray that the neighbours never learn what we are observing."
