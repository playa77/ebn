## WHAT THE DOMAIN TELLS NEWCOMERS

*The underground network is a system of tunnels, shafts, and stations connecting the domain's key locations beneath the city. The network includes decommissioned U-Bahn infrastructure, abandoned mining galleries, and service corridors that the domain maintains for transit and security purposes. Access to the network is restricted — routes are provided at acknowledgment on a need-to-know basis, and unauthorized exploration is prohibited. The Nosferatu maintain primary responsibility for the network's upkeep. Kindred who encounter the Nosferatu in the tunnels are advised to identify themselves, state their purpose, and follow any instructions they receive. The instructions are not negotiable.*

## THE KNOWN NETWORK

The underground network accessible to acknowledged Kindred consists of approximately twelve kilometers of tunnels and chambers, concentrated beneath the city center and extending north toward Katernberg and south toward the Baldeneysee. The network is not a single system. It is an accretion — layers of excavation from different periods, built for different purposes, connected by the Nosferatu into a functional transit infrastructure.

**The U-Bahn Layer.** The newest component, dating to the 1970s transit expansion. This includes the Ghost Station and the connecting corridors to the Hauptbahnhof, the Gildehof Center, and the Porscheplatz. These tunnels are modern — concrete, tile, municipal signage — and are maintained by the Keeper's office in cooperation with the Nosferatu. The U-Bahn layer is the only part of the network that visiting Kindred routinely access. It is also the shallowest — twenty to forty meters below the surface.

**The Mining Layer.** Deeper, older, denser. The coal mines that operated from the mid-nineteenth century through the 1980s produced a honeycomb of galleries, shafts, and ventilation tunnels beneath the city. The mining layer extends north from Katernberg (Zollverein) through the city center and east toward Kray. These tunnels are industrial — brick, timber, iron supports — and are maintained by the Nosferatu, who have converted the mining infrastructure into warden posts, transit corridors, and observation stations. The mining layer is the domain's operational underground. Most Kindred will never see it.

**The Pre-Industrial Layer.** The oldest component, fragmentary and inconsistently mapped. These are tunnels that predate the coal industry — some date to the medieval silver mining that began in 1354, some are older, and some are of unknown origin. The pre-industrial layer is not maintained. It is monitored. The Nosferatu warden posts are positioned at the intersections of the pre-industrial and mining layers, watching for changes in tunnels that were sealed before the domain existed.

## THE WARDEN SYSTEM

The Nosferatu maintain the underground network through a system of posts positioned at key junctions, access points, and anomaly boundaries. The system has been in continuous operation since at least the fourteenth century and possibly longer — the wardens' own records are not accessible to the surface domain, and the Nosferatu do not answer questions about their operational history.

A warden post consists of one or two Nosferatu stationed at a fixed location — a shaft head, a tunnel junction, a sealed gallery entrance — responsible for monitoring activity in their sector and reporting changes to the Schachtmeister. The posts are not defensive. They are observational. The wardens are not guarding against intrusion. They are watching for something that has been sealed for centuries and that the warden system was established to ensure remains sealed.

Warden posts known to the Seneschal's office:
- **Shaft 12** (Zollverein): Two wardens, continuous monitoring since 1986
- **Kray Extension** (Ghost Station): Two wardens, continuous monitoring since construction
- **Helene Colliery** (northern district): Two wardens, continuous monitoring since 1847 breach
- **Saalbau Depth** (city center): Two wardens, acoustic monitoring, occupied since at least 1970s
- **Gildehof Bunker** (city center): Operations center, unknown permanent complement
- **Old Silver Mine** (southern district): Rainer Voss, continuous monitoring since 1354

The warden system is not accountable to the domain. It is accountable to the Prince, through the Schachtmeister, and the Prince has never shared the terms of that accountability with anyone else. The domain tolerates this because the alternative is to govern the underground itself, and the domain's governance structure was not designed for underground governance. It was designed to keep the surface stable so the underground could remain sealed.

## ACCESS AND PROTOCOL

Kindred who require underground access — for transit, for investigation, for emergency shelter — apply to the Seneschal's office. The office grants access on a route-specific basis: a Kindred authorized to use the Gildehof-Ghost Station corridor is not authorized to enter the Kray Extension. A Kindred authorized to use the Kray Extension is not authorized to descend beyond the warden post.

Unauthorized exploration is prohibited. Enforcement is handled by the Nosferatu, who do not report enforcement actions to the Seneschal's office and whose enforcement methods are not documented. A Kindred who enters the underground without authorization and does not return is assumed to have encountered either a structural hazard or a warden. The domain does not investigate. The domain has learned not to ask.

The protocol is not punitive. It is protective. The underground contains things that the domain's institutional architecture was designed to keep at a distance — geological formations that are not natural, sealed chambers that are not empty, tunnels that were not dug by human hands and that lead to depths that no municipal survey has measured. A Kindred who enters the deep tunnels without warden escort is a Kindred who may encounter something the wardens have spent centuries ensuring no one encounters. The wardens' enforcement, however terminal, is preferable to the alternative.

## SEALED: THE SCHACHTMEISTER'S ASSESSMENT

*STORYTELLER ONLY*

The following was delivered to the Seneschal's office in 2022, as a supplement to the orientation brief. I have not edited it.

The underground network is not a transit system. It is not a security resource. It is a containment structure — a honeycomb of tunnels, shafts, and sealed galleries designed to encircle and isolate the anomaly beneath the central Ruhr. The network's primary function is not to move Kindred between surface locations. It is to maintain the perimeter of the sealed zone, to monitor the seals for degradation, and to ensure that the surface city never develops in directions that might compromise the containment.

The pre-industrial layer is the original containment system. The tunnels were dug — by whom, I cannot determine — to surround the anomaly with an observation perimeter. The mining layer intersected the pre-industrial layer in the nineteenth century, compromising the perimeter and requiring the warden system to adapt. The U-Bahn layer was built on top of both, and the Nosferatu used the construction to integrate the surface domain's infrastructure with the warden system, creating the Ghost Station, the Kray Extension, and the access routes that connect the surface to the underground.

The network's layout is not random. The tunnels trace the anomaly's boundary, forming a rough ellipse approximately eight kilometers by five, centered beneath the city. The boundary is not geological. It is geometric — too regular to be natural, too old to be human. The wardens' posts are positioned at the points where the boundary is thinnest, or most active, or most vulnerable to degradation. The Schachtmeister's responsibility is to monitor the boundary for change.

Change has been detected. The Opera Phenomenon — the music that plays in the Ghost Station and the Saalbau tunnel — has been increasing in intensity since the 2004 Philharmonie renovation. The Zollverein scan detected movement within the void. The warden posts at the boundary points have reported temperature anomalies, acoustic anomalies, and — in one instance, at the Helene post in 2018 — a pressure change consistent with something exhaling. The wardens have not used the word "exhaling." I have.

The network has been containing something for six hundred years or more. The containment is still holding. It is not holding as well as it was.

— S.
